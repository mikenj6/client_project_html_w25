ABOUT US

The University of Michigan offers food pantry services to support students, staff, and faculty experiencing food insecurity. These services provide access to essential groceries and personal care items at no cost. The Maize and Blue Cupboard, located in the basement of the Betsy Barbour Residence Hall, is a key resource, offering a variety of fresh produce, dairy, grains, proteins, and canned goods. Users can access the pantry on designated days and are encouraged to bring their own reusable bags. Additionally, the pantry often collaborates with campus partners and local organizations to ensure a well-stocked and diverse selection of items.The pantry also provides educational resources to help the university community make the most of the available food.Volunteer opportunities are available for those looking to support their peers and help with pantry operations. The goal is to create a supportive environment that fosters well-being and addresses food insecurity comprehensively.

OUR SERVICES

The Michigan Food Pantry offers a comprehensive suite of services aimed at alleviating hunger and promoting overall well-being. Our primary service, emergency food assistance, ensures that individuals and families facing immediate need receive a balanced variety of groceries—fresh fruits and vegetables, proteins, dairy products, and pantry staples. Beyond providing food, we offer nutrition education workshops designed to teach people how to prepare healthy, affordable meals. Our mobile pantry program expands our reach to underserved communities, bringing essential supplies directly to those who might not have the means or transportation to visit our locations. Additionally, we provide referral services, connecting clients to other resources available within our network to address the multifaceted challenges associated with poverty.

What we provide

The University of Michigan's Maize and Blue Cupboard offers a wide variety of food items to accommodate diverse dietary needs and preferences. The pantry typically stocks:

  • Fresh Produce: Seasonal fruits and vegetables that provide essential vitamins and nutrients.
  • Dairy Products: Items such as milk, cheese, and yogurt to support calcium and protein needs.
  • Grains: Whole grains, rice, pasta, and bread products for versatile meal options.
  • Proteins: A selection of plant-based and animal proteins, including beans, lentils, canned tuna, and chicken.
  • Canned and Packaged Goods: A variety of soups, sauces, and canned vegetables for convenient meal preparation.
  • Snacks: Healthy snacks, including granola bars, nuts, and dried fruits, for on-the-go nourishment.
  • Beverages: Options may include juice and shelf-stable milk to cater to different preferences.

Events

Attending or supporting events hosted by the Michigan Food Pantry is a great way to get involved and foster community spirit while significantly impacting those in need. Our calendar features a variety of events, including fundraising galas, community cook-offs, educational seminars, and health fairs. We also host seasonal and holiday-themed events, offering unique opportunities for community engagement and support. Each event is thoughtfully designed to raise awareness about food insecurity and generate essential funds for our services. Whether you attend, volunteer, or sponsor an event, your participation helps sustain and expand our critical programs. Keep an eye on our events page for the latest updates and opportunities to contribute to a meaningful cause.

Volunteer Opportunities

Volunteers are the backbone of the Michigan Food Pantry. Every year, hundreds of kind-hearted individuals devote their time and energy to help us collect, sort, package, and distribute food to those in need. We offer a variety of volunteering options to suit different schedules and interests—from organizing community drives and working in our distribution centers to conducting outreach, providing administrative support, and representing the pantry at public events. Whether you have a few hours or wish to commit to a weekly shift, your contribution can make a significant difference in our fight against hunger. We provide training and opportunities for personal growth, ensuring your volunteer experience is both rewarding and educational. Join our vibrant community of volunteers and help us create lasting change.
