What is CAPS?

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) at the University of Michigan is dedicated to enhancing and supporting the mental health and well-being of all students. Our team of licensed professionals offers a wide range of services designed to address the diverse psychological needs of our student body. We strive to create a safe, inclusive, and confidential environment where students can explore personal challenges, develop coping strategies, and enhance their overall emotional health. Learn more about our mission, guiding principles, and the compassionate team members committed to supporting students' mental health journeys.

What we do

CAPS provides a comprehensive suite of services to support the mental health needs of Michigan students. These include individual counseling, group therapy, workshops, and crisis intervention. Our individual counseling sessions are tailored to address personal, academic, and career concerns in a confidential setting. Group therapy offers a supportive community experience to explore common issues, while workshops focus on specific skills like stress reduction and mindfulness. In times of urgent need, our crisis intervention services ensure immediate support is available. We also offer medication management through our psychiatric services for students who may benefit from pharmacological interventions.

Making an Appointment

Scheduling an appointment with CAPS is a straightforward process designed to provide students with timely access to mental health support. Learn how to initiate counseling services, whether through a brief phone consultation, walk-in hours, or our online scheduling system. We also offer information on what to expect during your first visit, including initial assessments and discussions around confidentiality and treatment options. Our staff is committed to ensuring that all students receive the appropriate support quickly and conveniently, respecting your unique needs and schedule.

Resources and Self-Help

In addition to in-person services, CAPS provides a wide range of online resources and self-help tools to support students in managing their mental health. Access guides, apps, and online modules focusing on topics such as anxiety management, healthy relationships, and resilience building. Our curated resource library includes articles, recommended reading, and mindfulness exercises that students can engage with at their own pace. These resources are designed to empower students to take proactive steps toward personal growth and emotional well-being.

Workshops and Events

Our workshops and events are designed to enhance student well-being through education and community engagement. CAPS hosts a variety of workshops focusing on skill-building in areas such as stress management, emotional regulation, and communication. We also offer events during awareness weeks and special times of the year to address heightened mental health concerns, such as finals week. Check our calendar for upcoming workshops, drop-in sessions, and collaborative events with other campus organizations, and discover how these interactive experiences can contribute to your mental health journey.

Crisis and Emergency Support

Mental health crises can occur unexpectedly, and CAPS is committed to providing immediate support in such situations. Our crisis and emergency services include same-day crisis appointments, a 24/7 crisis line, and an in-person support team prepared to assist students facing urgent psychological distress. We work closely with campus safety and local emergency services to ensure that students receive comprehensive and immediate care. Learn more about recognizing signs of a mental health crisis and how to access our emergency support services whenever and wherever they are needed.

Contact us

Whether you're a student seeking support, a parent with questions, or a faculty member looking to collaborate, we invite you to reach out to us. Our contact page provides detailed information on how to get in touch with CAPS, whether through phone, email, or visiting our main office. We encourage you to connect with us for any inquiries, feedback, or support needs, and our staff will respond promptly and thoughtfully. Your mental health is our priority, and we are here to help in any way we can.
